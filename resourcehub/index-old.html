XR

Extended Reality (XR) is an umbrella term that comprises multiple forms of reality (Real, Virtual and Combining environments).

/Currently, this includes AR, AV, MR and VR.

/XR is sometimes also referred to as Cross-Reality, although Extended Reality is simply a more popular and commonly used name.

/There are countless devices, tools, platforms and applications used within XR. All of which keep rapidly evolving and thus frequently changing, making it challenging for designers, developers and enthusiasts to become a part of this field.

AR

Augmented Reality (AR) is a form of reality where virtual/digital objects are overlaid onto the real world.

/AR is used to make an experience interactive, most commonly through smartphones, browsers or recently emerging AR glasses.

/Some common examples for reference would be Pokemon Go, Live View on Google Maps, or filters on social media like Instagram, Snapchat and Tiktok.

/There are plenty of platforms and tools you can use to design/develop AR. Determining what product/object you want to launch in AR and how you want the users to interact with it, will help you decide what platform is best to use.

VR

Virtual Reality (VR) is fully immersing into a virtual environment with a computer simulated reality and headsets.

/VR is a completely immersive experience where the person interacts only with virtual/digital elements, and hence is best understood once experienced.

/VR uses multiple senses to create an interactive immersive space that generates realistic sounds, images and interactions, experienced via your headset.

/Some examples include a virtual places, playing games in VR, an underwater experience and experiences as seen in movies/shows like Ready Player One or Sword Art Online.

AV

Augmented Virtuality (AV) is placing and projecting real world objects, data and even people into a virtual world by scanning them.

/AV can be used to bring a greater collaboration of the real world into the virtual world.

/Slightly opposite to AR, real world objects are projected into the controlled virtual environment.

/Examples would be placing a project of yourself into a virtual space, putting a picture frame or lamp from your room into your virtual room, scanning a tree from the neighborhood and adding it to your virtual garden.

MR

Mixed Reality (MR) is a spatially aware form of reality that allows virtual objects to become a part of the real world.

/MR is a combination of AR and AV that makes use of spatial mapping to blend the real and virtual world.

/Using MR, you can create complex environments and have interactivity between the physical and digital elements.

/MR can act as a bridge between the real and virtual world and can be used via headsets/glasses that only partially cover your view of the real world.
